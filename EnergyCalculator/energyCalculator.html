About Energy Units

Joule (J)

The joule is the standard unit of energy in the International System of Units (SI). One joule equals the energy transferred when applying a force of one newton through a distance of one meter.

Power Units

Power units like kilowatt-hour (kWh) represent energy consumed over time. A kilowatt-hour is the energy consumed by a 1000-watt appliance running for one hour.

Mechanical Energy

Units like foot-pounds (ft⋅lb) and newton-meters (N⋅m) measure the energy transferred by applying a force over a distance.

Thermal Energy

BTU (British Thermal Unit) and calories measure heat energy. One BTU is the energy needed to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.
