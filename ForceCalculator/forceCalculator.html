About Force Units

SI Units

The SI unit of force is the newton (N), defined as the force needed to accelerate 1 kg at 1 m/s². Common prefixes include kilonewton (kN = 1000 N) and meganewton (MN = 1,000,000 N).

Imperial/US Units

Pound-force (lbf) is the force of gravity on one pound mass. 1 lbf ≈ 4.448 N. Kips (1000 lbf) are used in engineering. Poundals are an alternative unit where 1 pdl ≈ 0.138255 N.

Gravitational Units

Kilogram-force (kgf) is the force of Earth's gravity on 1 kg mass. 1 kgf = 9.80665 N. These units depend on standard gravity (g = 9.80665 m/s²).

Special Units

Dynes are CGS units (1 dyn = 10⁻⁵ N). The sthène is a MTS unit (1 sn = 1000 N). Standard atmosphere is a pressure unit but can express force when applied to an area.
