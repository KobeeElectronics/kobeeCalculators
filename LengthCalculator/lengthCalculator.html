About Length Units

Metric System

The metric system uses base-10 units. The meter (m) is the base unit, with prefixes indicating multiplication by powers of 10 (kilo-, centi-, milli-, etc.).

Imperial/US Units

Common in the US and UK, these units include inches, feet, yards, and miles. 1 mile = 5280 feet, 1 yard = 3 feet, 1 foot = 12 inches.

Maritime Units

Used in navigation, the nautical mile is based on the Earth's circumference. 1 nautical mile equals 1.852 kilometers or about 1.15078 statute miles.

Astronomical Units

Used for cosmic distances, light years measure the distance light travels in one year. Parsecs and astronomical units (AU) are also common in astronomy.
