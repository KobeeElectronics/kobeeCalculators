About Power Units

SI Units

The SI unit of power is the watt (W), defined as one joule per second. Common prefixes include kilowatt (kW = 1000 W), megawatt (MW = 1,000,000 W), and gigawatt (GW = 1,000,000,000 W).

Mechanical Power

Horsepower (hp) is commonly used for engines and motors. 1 hp ≈ 745.7 watts. Metric horsepower (PS) is slightly different at 735.5 watts. BTU/hour measures heating/cooling capacity.

Energy Rate Units

Power can be expressed as energy per time unit. Common examples include joules/second (same as watts), BTU/hour for HVAC systems, and calories/second for thermal applications.

Electrical Power

Volt-amperes (VA) measure apparent power in AC circuits. dBm is used in electronics, representing power relative to 1 milliwatt. 0 dBm = 1 mW, 30 dBm = 1 W.
