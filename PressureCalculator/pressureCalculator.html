About Pressure Units

SI Units

The Pascal (Pa) is the SI unit for pressure, defined as one newton per square meter (N/m²). Common multiples include kilopascal (kPa), megapascal (MPa), and bar (100,000 Pa).

Imperial/US Units

Pounds per square inch (psi) is widely used in the US and UK. Other units include inches of mercury (inHg) for atmospheric pressure and feet of water (ftH₂O) for hydraulic pressure.

Atmospheric Pressure

Standard atmospheric pressure is approximately 101,325 Pa, 1 atm, or 760 mmHg (torr). These units are commonly used in weather forecasting and altitude measurements.

Technical Units

Specialized units like kilogram-force per square centimeter (kgf/cm²) are used in engineering. The bar and its derivatives are common in industrial and meteorological applications.
