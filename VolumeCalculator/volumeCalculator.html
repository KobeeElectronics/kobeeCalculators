About Volume Units

Metric System

The metric system uses cubic meters (m³) as the base unit. Liters are commonly used, with 1 liter equal to 1 cubic decimeter (1L = 1dm³ = 0.001m³).

Imperial/US Liquid

US and Imperial gallons differ in size. Common units include gallons, quarts, pints, cups, and fluid ounces, with different conversion ratios between US and Imperial systems.

US Dry Measures

Used primarily for dry goods in the US. The system includes bushels, pecks, dry quarts, and dry pints, which are different from their liquid counterparts.

Special Units

Special volume units include oil barrels (42 US gallons) and beer barrels (31 US gallons), which are used in specific industries for measurement and trade.
