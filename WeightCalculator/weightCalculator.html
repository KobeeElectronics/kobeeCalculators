About Weight Units

Metric System

The SI unit for mass is the kilogram (kg). Common variations include grams (g) for smaller measurements and metric tons (t) for larger masses. 1 kg = 1000 g = 1,000,000 mg.

Imperial/US Units

Pounds (lb) and ounces (oz) are common in the US, while stone (st) is used in the UK. 1 lb = 16 oz, 1 st = 14 lb, and 1 US ton = 2000 lb.

Scientific Units

Daltons (Da) and atomic mass units (amu) are used in chemistry and physics for atomic/molecular masses. 1 Da = 1 amu ≈ 1.660539067 × 10⁻²⁷ kg.

Special Units

Carats (ct) are used for gemstones, where 1 ct = 0.2 g. Newtons (N) measure weight as force, where 1 kg weighs approximately 9.81 N on Earth.
